About

Darling Hydro Ltd is the hydropower consultancy business of Brian Darling. Brian is a chartered civil engineer with over 35 years’ experience in renewable energy, water, and environment sectors, with 20 of those years being focused on the hydropower industry. Particularly, Brian has been involved in leading multi-disciplinary teams in the development of viable hydropower projects. This has involved coordinating many facets to ensure they fit together from a technical, commercial, financial, environmental and social perspective.

Experienced in the following stages of work:

  • Strategic planning and concept studies
  • Pre-feasibility and feasibility studies
  • Tender and FEED Design
  • Detailed Design
  • Pre-qualification process
  • Tender documents and tender process
  • Contract and site supervision
  • Commissioning, testing and troubleshooting
  • Upgrade, refurbishment and retrofitting hydropower

Tender documents and tender process:

  • Instruction to tenderers (and request for bid instructions)
  • Employer’s Requirements or specifications
  • Reference design and design basis memorandum
  • Geological Baseline Report
  • Environmental, social and safety management and monitoring plans
  • Reference documents and drawings
  • Scoring mechanisms
  • Award negotiations and pre-contract clarification meetings

Brian has been involved in the above as Owner’s Engineer, Owner’s Designer, Contractor’s Designer, Lender’s Technical Advisor and Independent Engineer.
As hydropower engineer, Brian has been responsible for providing technical leadership to ensure a coherent hydropower project that draws together multi-disciplinary inputs, including:

Multi-disciplinary inputs

  • hydrology, water resource modelling and sediment management 
  • hydraulics, hydraulic modelling and flood assessments 
  • survey and remote sensing data 
  • scheme layout and optioneering 
  • power & energy modelling 
  • geology and geotechnical aspects
  • geotechnical investigation
  • seismic assessment
  • seepage, groundwater and grouting
  • grid integration studies
  • cost estimation 
  • construction planning
  • optimisation
  • risk analysis
  • health and safety considerations 
  • environmental and social impact assessment (ESIA)
  • environmental flow requirements (e-flows)
  • fish passes
  • financial and economic analysis
  • Building Information Modelling (BIM)

The above has included the following components:

  • Headworks and intake structures (including raised intakes),
  • Dams, coffer dams and flood embankments,
  • Spillways and cascades,
  • Inlet/outlet structures and associated gates,
  • Screens and trash booms,
  • Low-pressure and high-pressure tunnels (unlined, concrete lined, steel lined),
  • Pressure shafts and surge shafts,
  • Main access tunnels, emergency ventilated and cable tunnels and construction adits,
  • Power canals (including lining systems) and associated structures,
  • Penstocks and pipelines,
  • Surface and underground powerhouses,
  • Civil aspects of electro-mechanical plant and balance of plant,
  • Tailrace tunnels, tailrace channels and associated outlets,
  • Balancing storage and re-regulation structures,
  • Access roads and associated structures,
  • Site enabling works and temporary works,
  • Switchyard, power evacuation and transmission lines,
  • Local community infrastructure and local community interfacing.
